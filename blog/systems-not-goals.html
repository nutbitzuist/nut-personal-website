Why I Think in Systems, Not Goals

Nut (Sarunporn)
8 min read

For the first decade of my career in finance, I was obsessed with goals. Hit this return target. Close this deal. Reach this AUM milestone. And while goals took me far, they also left me stuck in a cycle of achievement → empty → next goal.

Then I discovered something that changed everything: systems thinking.

The Problem with Goals

Goals work when you're starting out. They give you direction and focus. But they have a fundamental flaw: goals are binary. You either hit them or you don't. And the moment you hit one, you need another to keep moving. It's a hamster wheel dressed up as progress.

In trading, this shows up as fixating on a specific PnL number. "I want to make $10K this month." What happens? You overtrade. You take bad setups. You let winning trades run too long or cut losers too late — because you're chasing a number, not following a process.

"You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems." — James Clear

What Systems Thinking Changed for Me

When I shifted from goal-oriented to systems-oriented thinking, three things happened:

  1. Consistency replaced intensity. Instead of big pushes followed by burnout, I built daily habits that compounded. Small improvements, every single day.
  2. Process became the product. I stopped measuring success by outcomes alone and started measuring by whether I followed my system. A losing trade taken correctly is a win. A winning trade taken recklessly is a loss.
  3. Scalability became possible. Goals don't scale. Systems do. Once I had a working trading system, I could run it across multiple instruments, timeframes, and accounts. The system does the work.

How I Apply This to Everything

In Trading

I built Expert Advisors — algorithmic trading robots — that codify my trading system into software. The EA doesn't have goals. It has rules. Entry conditions, exit conditions, position sizing, risk management. It executes the system perfectly, every time, without emotion.

In Business

Every business process I touch gets systematized. Onboarding workflows, content creation pipelines, financial reporting. If it can be documented, it can be automated. If it can be automated, it can scale.

In Personal Life

Morning routines. Weekly reviews. Quarterly planning sessions. I don't set vague goals like "get healthier." I build systems: "Walk 10,000 steps daily. Meal prep every Sunday. Sleep by 10:30 PM." The system runs and the results follow.

The 100x Leverage

Here's where it gets powerful. When you combine systems thinking with leverage — capital leverage, technology leverage, knowledge leverage — you don't just get linear improvements. You get exponential results.

A good trading system running on one account is nice. The same system running on 10 accounts across 5 brokers, managed by algorithms, monitored by dashboards? That's 100x.

That's what I mean by Leverage100x. Don't work 100x harder. Build systems that work 100x smarter.

Start Here

If you want to start thinking in systems, here's my recommendation:

  • Pick one area of your life or work that feels like a grind
  • Document exactly what you do, step by step
  • Identify which steps can be templated, automated, or delegated
  • Build v1 of your system — it doesn't have to be perfect
  • Run it for 30 days, measure results, iterate

The goal isn't perfection. The goal is to have a system that improves itself over time. That's the compounding edge that no single goal can give you.
